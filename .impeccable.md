## Design Context

### Users
Final demo-day visitors, investors, researchers, collaborators, and cohort members using the guide in-room on phones or before/after the event on desktop. Their job is to quickly understand the cohort, identify the right people and teams, orient around the program, and save promising conversations.

### Brand Personality
Editorial, strange, rigorous. The experience should feel like a field guide to a living research environment: confident and curious, never corporate or gamified.

### Aesthetic Direction
Match shaperotator.xyz's actual brand language: Space Grotesk and JetBrains Mono, charcoal ink, warm paper/cream surfaces, and oxide red as the only accent. For this visitor utility, invert the landing page's dark threshold into its warm paper token palette while retaining its editorial marginalia, wireframe geometry, restrained rounding, and scientific-instrument tone. Avoid the desktop OS's dark application chrome, generic dashboard cards, gradients, emoji icons, and excessive glass effects.

### Design Principles
1. Treat the cohort as a connected field, not a directory.
2. Make the next useful conversation obvious within seconds.
3. Use one accent and let typography, rules, and negative space carry hierarchy.
4. Adapt interaction architecture by device: bottom tabs and sheets on mobile, generous workspace and top navigation on desktop.
5. Make every interaction keyboard-visible, touch-safe, and reduced-motion aware.
